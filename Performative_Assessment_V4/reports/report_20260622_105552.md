**Expert guidance:**
> Move the vehicle completely off the road onto a stable, flat surface. Turn on hazard lights immediately and apply the parking brake. Retrieve the spare tire, jack, and lug wrench from the trunk. With the flat still on the ground, break the lug nuts loose — do not remove them yet. Locate the manufacturer's designated jack point under the frame (consult the owner's manual if needed) and position the jack there. Raise the vehicle until the flat tire is a few inches off the ground. Remove the lug nuts fully and pull off the flat. Mount the spare tire and hand-tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern. Lower the vehicle until the spare just contacts the ground, then torque the lug nuts fully in a star pattern. Lower completely and remove the jack. Check the spare's pressure with a gauge before driving. Stow all equipment and the flat in the trunk. Drive at or below 50 mph and reach a tire shop within 50–70 miles — compact spares are not rated for extended use.
